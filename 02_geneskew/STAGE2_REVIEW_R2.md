## (a) Did the fixes land?

**What landed cleanly — stop worrying about these:**

- **G1/G2/G3/G4 as a pre-screen gate tier.** This is exactly the reframe I was asking for, and structurally it's right: the axis-quality gates now mirror the per-perturbation power tiers, and §1.3 correctly identifies that the *default* passes them trivially while a user picking two nearby substates is the failure mode they catch. G3's explicit `sign_validated: true|false` carried into the output schema (§3) is the honest move. G4 correctly states the cosine guard can *invert* rather than merely weaken.
- **The NTC-null refinements** (§2.5) — recomputed-per-contrast, and the parametric fallback when `n_NTC` can't support the 3,341-way tail — both landed.
- **Lane C mechanism correction.** "The OT coupling is noise→data, not control→perturbed and not A→B" is correct, and "difference of two generated conditionals … retains a mean-shift character and does not magically transcend the measured lane's limits" is the right conclusion. The reframe of DE-concordance from evidence-to-harvest into a **model-QC gate you must pass**, the lane's own null, the R9b HVG-coverage check, R9's inherits-the-confounds caveat, the combination-KD-is-OOD flag, and the no-CPU-inference/remote-GPU dependency — all present. You also picked up my narrower point that the in-silico contrast must be anchored on **source = A**, not scored as "closeness to B" alone. That's a genuinely complete rewrite.

**What half-landed — these are the ones I'd push on:**

**1. R2b's mitigation (b) is quietly dangerous *for this specific default*, and it collides with G4.** You list, in preference order, (a) common-timepoint poles, (b) "regress an explicit activation-duration/kinetics axis out of `w`," (c) verify hits aren't sliding along the trajectory. The problem: because A is defined at 48h and B at 8h, the activation-duration axis and the fate axis are **partially collinear by construction** — 8h→48h activation *is* substantially a proliferation/translation/metabolic ramp. So (b) doesn't cleanly remove a nuisance direction; it removes a direction that overlaps the real Treg-vs-Th1 signal, and you can gut the axis you're trying to build. Worse, G4's "project out a proliferation/translation reference direction" is *nearly the same operation* for this contrast — so you have two named fixes (R2b-b and G4) that are near-duplicates here, and running both risks over-projection. The memo treats these as three independent, freely-composable knobs; for the default they aren't. Two things I'd add: (i) state that for the shipped default, the kinetics axis (R2b) and the broad-proliferation axis (G4) are not independent, so project *one* combined nuisance direction, not both; and (ii) note that mitigation (a) — the preferred one — **may not be available**: it depends on whether induced-Treg and Th1 actually co-occur in usable numbers at a single timepoint, which is an empirical question, not a given. If they don't, you're forced onto the confounded (b) or the check-only (c), and the confound is only *partially* addressable. Right now (a) reads as a clean escape hatch that may be closed.

**2. G2's A∪B-fraction is ill-defined across the split-timepoint poles — this is the interaction R2b and G2 don't close.** G2 says "compute the fraction of the *scored condition's* cells belonging to A∪B." But the screen scores **Stim48hr** DE, A (induced-Treg) lives at 48h, and B (Th1) is "best expressed in the 8h compartment." So in the scored condition, the B-fraction may be ≈0 and A∪B ≈ the 6.3% A alone. The proxy-quality number you're reporting is then really "A-fraction," and the "B" half of the A→B skew has almost no cells in the condition where you measure the perturbation effect. That's not a reason to drop G2 — it's a reason to **specify which condition(s) the fraction is computed over and report A and B fractions separately**, and to say plainly that with split-timepoint poles the single A∪B number is an incomplete proxy. As written, G2 inherits R2b's confound without acknowledging it, and a reader will take a "6.3%" A∪B number at face value when the B side is essentially unrepresented in the scored data.

**3. The corrected Lane C mechanism is right in the headline but the operational bullet still carries A→B-transport residue.** In §2.8 the in-silico-SkewScore bullet says: "generate the **B-context conditional** under knockdown vs under control (**anchored on the A/source context**)." That sentence conflates the two things the mechanism correction was supposed to separate — it names both a B-context conditional *and* an A/source anchor, which can't both be the conditioning context. The correct counterfactual under noise→data conditional generation is unambiguous: **condition on the A/source context (its donor/timepoint), generate under KD vs control, take the difference vector, then project that onto the A→B axis.** There is no "B-context conditional" in that operation. The headline reframe landed; the recipe underneath it still reads as if you're generating something at B and pulling it back toward A. I'd rewrite that one bullet to remove "B-context conditional" entirely — otherwise the lane's implementation will re-import the transport thinking you just corrected.

**4. The scLDM provenance numbers moved from "unconfirmed" to "asserted" — confirm they're actually *in* Zhu et al.** I'd flagged 14.5M cells / 3,699 HVGs / the 15.2M+44.3M param counts as numbers I couldn't verify from public docs, and asked you to pin-or-soften. The memo now states them as facts and cites Zhu et al. 2025 (bioRxiv 2025.12.23.696273). That's the right *form* of the fix (pin to source), but I can't see from here whether those specific figures come from that preprint or from adjacent material. Worth one check that each number traces to that citation before it hardens into a provenance block downstream.

## (b) Is the Stage-2 / Stage-3–4 boundary in the right place?

Broadly, **yes** — "Stage-2 stops at the ranked, gated candidate-gene table" is the right cut, it's stated as a hard boundary, and the output schema (§3) plus the portable program registry give Stage-3 what it needs to do the gene- and program-level joins. Folding the addendum's Part A into §1.4 is clean, and scoping the six tumor/drug/brain evidence axes out to §4 is the correct move. Three issues, one of them concrete:

**1. Essentiality is mis-deferred — at least the flat common-essential tag belongs in Stage-2, and the data is already sitting in the project.** Your own project principle makes "separate specific program-shifters from essential-gene sledgehammers" a first-class Stage-2 duty, and G4 just told us the *internal* discriminator (cosine) can invert for broad axes. Yet §2.2 defers the essentiality cross-check to "DepMap common-essential in Stage-3." But there's a `CEGv2.txt` artifact already in this project — the Hart Core Essential Genes v2 list, 684 genes with HGNC/Entrez IDs — which is a **static common-essential annotation needing no DepMap grant and no network access at all.** So the sledgehammer flag has an independent, cheap, available-now backstop that doesn't depend on the cosine heuristic. I'd tag core-essential membership *in Stage-2* using CEGv2 (an explicit `core_essential` flag alongside the cosine/breadth tags), and defer only the **glioma-*selective* Chronos dependency** — the quantitative, grant-requiring part — to Stage-3. As drawn, the boundary sends the entire essentiality question downstream, which weakens the one separation your principles say Stage-2 must own.

**2. Folding `03_tumor_context` into `03_druglink`'s front half loses the stage-boundary gate the addendum used to enforce "relevance before chemistry."** The addendum argued for a *separate* tumor-context stage precisely so a brain-penetrant-but-GBM-irrelevant target couldn't survive on chemistry points. Collapsing it into 03 to avoid renumbering the locked 01→05 architecture is a reasonable call — but the "relevance gate precedes druggability" discipline now lives *inside* one stage as an ordering convention, not as a hard stage boundary. I'd make that intra-stage sequencing explicit in the §4 text: within 03, the tumor-context/compartment relevance filter runs and gates *before* tractability scoring, so a candidate can't be carried on druggability alone. Right now §4 asserts the 03-before-04 ordering (relevance+drug before penetrance+safety) but doesn't re-guarantee the relevance-before-drug ordering that used to be a stage wall.

**3. A safety axis is genuinely missing and unhomed: systemic/T-cell-intrinsic immune safety.** The whole Stage-2 mechanism is "KD that reverses induced-Treg and de-represses inflammatory Th1." Systemically, that is *exactly* the direction of autoimmune liability — it's the mechanistic neighbor of checkpoint-inhibitor irAEs. The deferred §4 scorecard has "normal-brain safety" (GTEx/HPA CNS tox) but no axis for **breaking peripheral tolerance / autoimmune risk from disrupting Treg programs**. That's not a Stage-2 computation, but it's a first-class safety axis for this specific mechanism and it currently has no home in Stages 3–4. I'd add it to §4 explicitly (even as a qualitative/literature axis), tagged to the immune/microenvironment branch, rather than let it fall through the gap between "normal-brain tox" and "tumor relevance."

## If I'd change only a few things

1. Rewrite the R2b/G4 interaction: for the default, the kinetics and proliferation nuisance directions are collinear — project one combined direction, not two — and flag that the preferred common-timepoint mitigation may not exist in the data.
2. Make G2 report A- and B-fractions separately over named conditions, and acknowledge the B side is barely present in the scored 48h condition.
3. Fix the one Lane C bullet that still says "B-context conditional anchored on A" — condition on A, generate KD-vs-control, project onto the axis.
4. Pull core-essential tagging (CEGv2, already in-project) into Stage-2; defer only glioma-selective Chronos to Stage-3.
5. Add an autoimmune/systemic-immune-safety axis to §4 and make 03's relevance-before-druggability ordering explicit.

None of this touches the architecture — the gate tier, the two-lane split, the firewall, the file chain, and the scope boundary are all sound. These are the places where a fix half-landed or a real axis slipped through the boundary you just drew.
