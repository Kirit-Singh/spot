How this map was built

    
  

  

    
1

      
Data source

      
≈396,000 non-targeting-control CD4 T cells from the Marson GWCD4i CRISPRi perturb-seq; 4 donors × Rest / Stim 8 hr / Stim 48 hr. Public on the CZI Virtual Cell Platform, pulled via the vcp CLI. 40,000-cell subsample of the scVI embedding shown.

    

    
2

      
Clustering & annotation

      
scVI at the authors' parameters (on raw counts) → Leiden-0.8 → 13 clusters. The clustering and the state-program labels (naïve-like / activated / cycling / adhesion-high / treg-like) are the workbench's own display keys — the authors' NTC notebook embeds but doesn't cluster the cells, and Marson defines no cell-state taxonomy (it clusters the 3,341 perturbations, not cells). Each cluster's label is just the argmax of continuous, within-dataset z-scored program scores; it is a display convenience, not a cell-type assignment.

    

    
3

      
Continuous program scores

      
Every cell carries continuous transcriptional-program compatibility scores (7 functional programs — Th1/2/17-like, Tfh-like, Th9-like, Treg-like, CD4 CTL-like — and 4 differentiation-state scores). These are RNA-signature similarities, not categorical calls: a high Treg-like or CD4 CTL-like score does not demonstrate lineage stability, protein expression, cytotoxicity, or suppressive function. On 10x Flex (probe-based) data, dropout of low-abundance transcripts (e.g. FOXP3) is expected, so scores are read as continuous gradients, never thresholded into cell types.

    

    
4

      
Program scoring

      
Per-cell scoring against the Masopust et al. helper-program signatures, expressed as continuous within-dataset z-scores (no thresholds, no discrete calls). Activation is a known confound for the Treg-like and CD4 CTL-like programs, so an activation-adjusted CD4 CTL-like score (cd4_ctl_like_score_actadj) is reported alongside the raw score for context. All values are exploratory and directional only.

    

    
5

      
Scope & limits

      
Reviewed with Claude Science against the raw data and both papers. What this map is — and is not:

      
Continuous scores, not calls. Every colour and number here is a continuous transcriptional-program compatibility score. Nothing on this page asserts a validated cell type, lineage, or function.

      
Activation is confounded with several programs. Treg-like and CD4 CTL-like signatures share genes that switch on with any activation, so raw scores partly track activation state. The activation-adjusted CD4 CTL-like score is provided for context; neither version is a cytotoxicity or suppression measurement.

      
External validation required. This rests on one in-vitro, 4-donor CD4 dataset. RNA program-compatibility can suggest hypotheses but cannot confirm protein expression, cytotoxicity, suppressive function, or lineage stability. Treat all programs as suggestive pending orthogonal (protein / functional) confirmation.
