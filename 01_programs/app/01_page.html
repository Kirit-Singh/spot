How this map was built

    
  

  

    
1

      
Data source

      
≈396,000 non-targeting-control CD4 T cells from the Marson GWCD4i CRISPRi perturb-seq; 4 donors × Rest / Stim 8 hr / Stim 48 hr. Public on the CZI Virtual Cell Platform, pulled via the vcp CLI. 40,000-cell subsample of the scVI embedding shown.

    

    
2

      
Clustering & annotation

      
scVI at the authors' parameters (on raw counts) → Leiden-0.8 → 13 clusters. The clustering and the 5 state-program names (Naïve / Activated / Cycling / Resting / Treg) are the workbench's own — the authors' NTC notebook embeds but doesn't cluster the cells, and Marson defines no cell-state taxonomy (it clusters the 3,341 perturbations, not cells). Labels are assigned by a fixed, confound-aware marker rule (label_clusters.py), reproducible from the raw object.

    

    
3

      
State labels

      
Naïve and Resting are quiescent, naïve-derived; Cycling is bimodal — naïve/resting-like at rest, activated at 48 hr (cell cycle is orthogonal to lineage). The former "Memory" cluster is labelled Resting: 3′ scRNA-seq cannot resolve the CD45RA/CD45RO switch, and the Masopust "M" call rests on non-specific adhesion markers (CD11a/CD95/CD58). It is not Tscm — SELL/TCF7/LEF1 sit at ~half the naïve level and CD95 is not elevated (a downshift out of naïve, not a stem-memory gain; memory can't form in ≤48 hr in vitro). Activated splits by timepoint (8 hr Th1-skew, 48 hr no skew); Treg is a discrete cluster.

    

    
4

      
Function calls

      
Per-cell scoring against the Masopust et al. helper-lineage signatures behind a permutation-FDR floor (500 shuffled-gene nulls, BH q<0.05). Activation confounds are handled per lineage: CD25/IL2RA dropped from Treg; CD4-CTL keeps its full cytotoxic panel (GNLY/PRF1/GZMH/KLRD1/GZMB/NKG7) with the activation score regressed out. 82% of cells return no call (polyclonal Th0 stimulation); coherent skews appear at 8 hr (Th1) and in the Treg cluster.

    

    
5

      
Review & validation

      
Reviewed with Claude Science against the raw data and both papers. Two labelling decisions tested against the numbers:

      
"Resting", not Tscm. Stemness markers are lower than naïve (SELL 37% vs 76%, TCF7 20% vs 44%, LEF1 51% vs 79%) and CD95/FAS is not elevated — a downshift out of naïve, not a stem-memory gain.

      
How activation confounds are handled. Treg and CD4-CTL both carry markers that switch on with any activation (CD25 for Treg; GZMB/NKG7 for CD4-CTL), which would tag ordinary activated cells. They resolve oppositely: re-including CD25 collapses Treg call coherence (55→30%) with no gain, so it's dropped. For CD4-CTL, GZMB/PRF1/NKG7 are core cytotoxic effectors, so rather than discard them we regress out the activation component — roughly doubling real CTL calls (11→24%) at comparable coherence. (This adds ~1% spurious CD4-CTL calls in resting cells — a non-target lineage.)

      
Treg = cluster, not the FDR call. The per-cell FDR function-Treg (2.7%) and the Leiden Treg cluster (6.3%) overlap by only Jaccard 0.08. FOXP3 is captured in just 28% of cluster-Tregs (3′ dropout of a low-abundance TF), so the FDR gate rejects real Tregs for a missing FOXP3 read, not for being less Treg — it's detection-biased, not purer. The whole-transcriptome cluster is the primary Treg definition; the FDR call is kept only as a caveated secondary. What it is: activation-induced FOXP3⁺ regulatory-like cells at 48 hr, not natural Tregs — FOXP3⁺CTLA4⁺ rises ~26× Rest→48 hr while Helios/IKZF2 stays flat, and the cluster is ~99% Stim48hr (one resting FOXP3⁺ cell falls in it). Stage-2 reads its hits as modulators of induced FOXP3.
